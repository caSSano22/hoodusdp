how_it_works

Three taps. No wallet in sight.

Sending money on-chain shouldn't feel like on-chain. HOOD USDP hides the addresses, the keys, and the amounts — you just enter a number and hit send.

📱
01

Enter a phone number

Type any phone number like you're sending a text. HOOD USDP routes it to the recipient's private on-chain account — creating one for them if they don't have it yet.

🔒
02

Amount gets encrypted

The value is sealed with fully homomorphic encryption before it ever hits the chain. A zero-knowledge proof confirms the transfer is valid without revealing how much moved.

✅
03

They just get paid

The recipient gets a text with a claim link. No app download, no seed phrase, no gas. They cash out to Robinhood, a bank, or hold it as USDG.

privacy_by_default

Public rails. Private amounts.

Most stablecoin transfers broadcast exactly how much you sent, to whom, forever. HOOD USDP keeps the settlement on a public chain but seals the sensitive parts — so your payment history stays yours.

FHE

Encrypted amounts

Balances and transfer values are computed while still encrypted — the network verifies math it can't read.

ZK

Zero-knowledge validity

Each payment carries a proof that it's funded and correct, without exposing sender, receiver, or value.

#

Phone-routed identity

A number maps to a stealth account. No public address to dox, track, or link back to you.

faq

The short version.

Do recipients need a crypto wallet?

No. A recipient claims funds from a text link. HOOD USDP creates a private on-chain account for them automatically — they never touch a seed phrase.

Can anyone see how much I sent?

No. Amounts are encrypted with FHE and validated with zero-knowledge proofs. The transfer settles publicly, but the value, sender, and receiver stay hidden.

What is USDG?

USDG is a fully-backed stablecoin. Balances in HOOD USDP are held and moved as USDG, so a dollar stays a dollar.

Which chain does it run on?

Robinhood Chain. Settlement is fast and low-cost, and there's no gas for the person receiving a payment.
